Spatial Distribution of Traumatic Brain Injury Contusions
and Post-Traumatic Epilepsy

Daniel Brennan PhD1, Andrea L.C. Schneider MD PhD1, James J. Gugger MD PharmD2


1 University of Pennsylvania Department of Neurology, Philadelphia PA, United States
2 University of Rochester Department of Neurology, Rochester NY, United States

Background

  • Focal traumatic brain injury (TBI) and contusions are associated with post-traumatic epilepsy (PTE)
  • Observed differences confounded by increased injury severity
  • Unclear whether focal injury related beyond injury severity, which contusion characteristics (core/edema) associated

Objective: To characterize the spatial distribution of TBI contusions in PTE

Methods

  • Transforming Research and Clinical Knowledge in TBI Epileptogenesis Project (TRACK-TBI EPI) data
  • Manual contusions segmentation on 2-week T1/FLAIR MRI; masks warped to MNI space
  • Disconnection mapping: extend contusion via intersection of normative white-matter tract atlas
  • LESYMAP: multivariate contusion map analysis
  • Cosine-similarity of stat-map & contusions compared with covariates: age, sex, GCS, total-contusion-volume

Conclusions

  • Contusions burden increased in PTE beyond injury severity - need for external validation
  • White matter disconnected by contusions represents strong candidate biomarker
  • Contusion-disconnected white matter may carry PTE relevant signal beyond focal injury burden
